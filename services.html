Paid Search Management

Paid Search on Google and Bing is where I first started in the industry and where I have built most of my professional reputation. These days it represents sllightly less than 40% of my business; this is because it is difficult to get a good client fit for ongoing account management for the following reasons:

  1. An account must be large enough for me to make a big enough difference. For example if I improve a small £1000/month account by 15% this is an extra £150/month which will not cover my fee let alone provide a good ROI on the engagement for the business owner.
  2. An account must be small enough that I can manage it by myself. A 15% improvement on a £1,000,000/month account is £150,000 which would provide an excellent return on my fee for the business. But it is unlikely that I would be able to do a good job of managing an account of this size by myself.
  3. The above two points have always been true in the PPC industry but over the last few years the quality of automated tooling and the quality of new (cheap) graduate entrants into online marketing has increased the size of account for which I am good value; there are accounts where I can do a good job and more than cover my fee but I don't do well enough to differentiate myself from a younger, cheaper person working with good automation.
This means I am a good person to work with on accounts where inexperienced practicioners or typical automation tools perform poorly. This happens a lot when the goals of the account are not easily captured in web analytics tools. For example, an ecommerce store optimising for revenue is well served by many automated tools and any PPC practicioner can tell if their optimisations are having the desired effect.

A more complicated example where I would expect to have more impact would be a hotel looking to increase bookings. There are only a limited number of rooms and at some times of year these rooms will sell without any paid marketing effort but at other times of year occupancy will be low. The challenge is to increase occupancy at the slack times without paying for non-incremental bookings at the busy times. Tooling for this problem is not well developed (yet).

Paid Search Audits

A lot of the obstacles to finding a good client fit for Paid Search Management do not apply for Paid Search Audits. As a one off expense the cost can be amortised over a longer period of time which means it is more viable for small accounts. And the workload is much easier to plan which means that given the right deadline I can audit even the largest of accounts.

And, as a bonus, I love digging into new accounts and new businesses to figure out how it all works and how it all fits together.

I have done audits for in house teams and agency teams to provide a fresh pair of eyes and some new ideas. I have also audited "from the other side" to evaluate agency performance and whether or not the client is getting value for money.
